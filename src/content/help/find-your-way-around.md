TTN Chords has two sides: an everyday side for choosing songs and playing along,
and an authoring side for creating and managing the songbook. This guide shows
where things live and how to switch between the two.

## The main areas

Use the sidebar (on a computer) or the bottom bar (on a phone):

- **Songs** — your songbook. Open a song to read or perform it.
- **Setlists** — ordered groups of songs for a class or session.
- **Reports** — printable songbooks and handouts.
- **Tools** — the metronome and tuner.
- **Settings** — appearance, your instrument, the starter library, backup, and
  this Help.

## Everyday vs. authoring mode

By default the app is set up for playing: you can open songs, perform them, use
the tools, and build setlists. Creating and editing songs is hidden behind
**authoring (admin) mode** so the everyday view stays simple.

If you're the person who maintains the songbook, turn that on — see
[Turn on authoring (admin) mode](/help/turn-on-admin-mode).

## What you'll see

With authoring mode off you'll see **Perform** buttons and the music tools, but
no **New**, **Edit**, or **Tag beats** buttons. Turn authoring on and those
appear throughout the app.
