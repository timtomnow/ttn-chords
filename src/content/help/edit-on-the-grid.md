The beat grid lets you fine-tune exactly where each chord and word falls. You can
nudge a chord onto the right beat, fix a lyric that was cut off, split a word so
it starts on its own beat, or drop in a missing word. Everything you change here
flows back to the song, so the Scroll and Lyrics views, printed reports, and
exports all stay in sync.

## Before you start

- The song needs beat timing. If it has none yet, tag it first — see
  [Tag the beats of a song](/help/tag-beats).
- You can open the grid from two places, which work the same way:
  - the **Highway** view's **Edit** button (the pencil), or
  - the fine-tune step at the end of **Tag beats**.

## Steps

1. Open the grid (Highway → **Edit**, or finish a **Tag beats** take).
2. **Move** a chord or word: drag its marker along the grid. Tap a marker to
   select it, then use **−** / **+** to nudge it earlier or later, or the eraser
   to clear its beat. Set the snap with **1/4**, **1/8**, or **1/16**.
3. **Edit a word:** click the lyric beneath a chord, change the text, and press
   **Enter**. Clearing the text removes that word.
4. **Split a word:** click the lyric, place the cursor where the split should be,
   and tap **Split**. The second half becomes its own marker — drag it onto the
   beat where that word actually starts.
5. **Add a word:** tap **＋ Lyric**, then click the grid where the word belongs
   and type it. It lands on its own beat with no chord attached.
6. **Add a chord** (Highway only): tap **＋ Chord**, then click the grid for a
   beat-only chord, or click a letter in a lyric to anchor a chord over it.
7. Insert a bar with **＋** between bars (this pushes the rest of the song over);
   empty bars show a trash icon to delete them.
8. Leave **Edit**, or tap **Save** in Tag beats, to keep your changes.

## What you'll see

Words sit beneath their chords on the grid; a word on its own beat shows a small
dot (·) marker you can drag like a chord. Because edits are saved to the song
itself, switching to the **Scroll** or **Lyrics** view — or exporting the song —
shows the same updated chords and lyrics.
