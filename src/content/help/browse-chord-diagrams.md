The chord diagrams browser shows all of an instrument's chord charts in one
place. Pick an instrument, narrow the list with the filters, and reorder it to
suit how you think about chords. You can also add, customize, or remove charts
right here — no admin mode required.

## Steps

1. Open **Tools** and tap **Chord Diagrams**.
2. Tap an instrument chip (Guitar, Ukulele, Bass, Piano, or a custom one) to
   switch what's shown. It opens on your current instrument.
3. Narrow and reorder the charts with the controls at the top:
   - **Key** — show only the chords that belong to a major key (e.g. *C major*).
     Leave it on **All keys** to see everything.
   - **Sort** — **Default order**, **Alphabetical**, **By root (chromatic)**, or
     **Circle of fifths**.
   - **Show 7th & extended chords** — untick to see just the basic triads.
4. To add your own chart, tap **Add chord**, enter the chord name, and set the
   shape.
5. To tweak a bundled shape, hover (or focus) a **Standard** chart and tap the
   pencil — it opens pre-filled so you can save your own version.
6. On a **Custom** chart, use the pencil to edit or the trash icon to delete it.

## What you'll see

A grid of chord diagrams for the chosen instrument. **Standard** holds the
shapes that ship with the app; **Custom** holds the ones you've added. The
filters and sort apply to both. A custom chart replaces the bundled shape for
that chord everywhere it's shown.
